## 2. Customer interview script (Mom-Test style — never pitch)

Goal: learn about their life, not validate Oxy. Talk 20%, listen 80%. Don't mention the product until the very end.

**Open (30s):** "Thanks for the time. I'm researching how people handle stuff on the go — I'm not selling anything, I just want to hear how *you* actually do things. Mind if I ask about your day?"

**The questions (follow the energy, don't read robotically):**
1. "Walk me through the last time you needed to do something — send a message, check a plan, set a reminder — while your hands were full or you were out and about. What happened?"
2. "How often does that kind of thing come up?"
3. "What did you do? Did it work?" *(dig: what was annoying about it?)*
4. "Have you ever tried anything to make that easier?" *(Siri? a smartwatch? nothing?)* "How'd that go?" *(this reveals if the pain is real — real pain = they've tried to fix it)*
5. "What's the most annoying part of getting things done on your phone when you're busy?"
6. "If you could wave a wand and have one thing happen hands-free, what would it be?"
7. "Last thing — do you wear anything every day? Watch, jewellery, earbuds? What made you choose it?" *(reads the fashion-object angle)*

**Only at the very end:** "I'm building a little voice assistant pendant around exactly this. Can I keep you posted / would you want early access?" → if yes, that's a real signal. Add to waitlist.

**Log per interview (Notion):** pain real? (Y/N) · tried-before? (Y/N) · would they pay? · best quote.

**Gate:** ≥40% describe the pain unprompted + have tried to solve it before.
